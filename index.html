The history of memory

Every generation found a new way
to preserve the people they loved.

The Portrait
1400s – 1800s
Oil paintings preserved faces for the wealthy. A commission could take months. Only the powerful left a visual trace.
The Photograph
1839 – 1980s
Photography democratised memory. Suddenly everyone could preserve a moment. Weddings, graduations, ordinary Tuesdays — all frozen in time.
The Film
1890s – 2000s
Cine cameras preserved movement — how someone walked, laughed, gestured. For the first time, the dead could be seen in motion.
The Digital Life
2000s – Today
For the first time, we can preserve the whole person — their humour, values, music, stories, relationships, voice. Every dimension of a life, not just its surface.

Now — Forever

📱 How to Export Contacts from Your Phone
📱 iPhone (iOS)
  1. Open iCloud.com on your computer
  2. Sign in with your Apple ID
  3. Click Contacts
  4. Click the gear icon (⚙️) → Select All
  5. Click the gear again → Export vCard
  6. Upload the .vcf file here
🤖 Android
  1. Open Contacts app
  2. Tap the 3-dot menu (⋮)
  3. Select Settings → Export
  4. Choose Export to .vcf file
  5. Save to your device
  6. Upload the .vcf file here

💙 About We Loved Life

"We didn't build this for users. We built it for grandchildren who haven't been born yet."

We Loved Life was founded by a team of senior technologists spanning Europe and Australia — people who have spent careers building software that millions rely on. Between us we've led engineering teams, shipped AI products, and helped scale companies from startup to enterprise.

But this project started with something personal. The realisation that the people we love most leave the smallest digital trace. A few photos. Some messages that will one day vanish from someone's phone. Nothing that captures who they actually were — their laugh, their advice, the songs that soundtracked their life, the lessons they'd have wanted to pass on.

Our ethos is simple: make great software that people actually want to use. No dark patterns, no attention traps, no data selling. Just a beautifully crafted space to tell your story — built with the same rigour and care we'd bring to any enterprise product, but designed for something far more important.

We Loved Life is currently in free beta. We're building it in the open, listening to every piece of feedback, and we'd love to hear from you.

Privacy Policy

Last updated February 2026

1. What We Collect

We collect your name, email address, and the personal content you choose to store — contacts, photos, stories, music preferences, documents and messages. We also collect standard technical data such as device type and approximate location for security purposes.

2. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used solely to provide the We Loved Life service — storing your memories and making them available to the people you designate. We do not use your content for advertising, AI training, or any commercial purpose. We never read your personal entries.

3. Who Can See Your Data

Only you and the inheritors you explicitly designate can access your capsule. We Loved Life staff cannot read your capsule contents. Anonymised, aggregated statistics (e.g. number of users) may be used internally to improve the product.

4. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored on enterprise-grade infrastructure (AWS and Google Cloud) with AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. We use Supabase as our database provider, which is SOC 2 Type 2 certified.

5. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, all your data is permanently deleted within 30 days. You can request a full export of your data at any time by emailing [email protected].

6. Third Parties

We use Supabase (database), Google Analytics (anonymous site usage statistics), and optionally Spotify (music data you choose to import). We do not sell your data to any third party.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, export or delete your personal data at any time. To exercise these rights, email [email protected]. If you are in the EU or UK, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. We will notify registered users of any material changes by email. Continued use of the service after changes constitutes acceptance.
